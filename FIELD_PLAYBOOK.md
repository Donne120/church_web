## ðŸšª Phase 1: Getting the "Open Door" (Permission)

### Timeline: 1-2 weeks before outreach

### Step 1: Research the Campus

**Find the Right Gatekeeper:**
- Dean of Students Office
- Chaplaincy/Campus Ministry
- Students Guild/Union
- Clubs & Societies Office
- Student Affairs Department

**What to Find Out:**
- Who approves student activities?
- Is club registration required?
- What's the approval process?
- Are there any restrictions?
- Best days/times for events?

### Step 2: Prepare Your Request

**Create a One-Pager Including:**
1. **Who We Are**: CYSMF - Christian Youth and Students Missionary Fellowship
2. **Our Mission**: Reaching students with the Gospel, discipleship, leadership
3. **What We Want**: Permission for campus outreach/fellowship meetings
4. **Proposed Activities**:
   - Gospel presentations
   - Testimonies & worship
   - Tract distribution
   - Small group discipleship
   - Leadership training
5. **Dates & Times**: Specific proposals
6. **Safety & Compliance**:
   - No disruption to classes
   - Respect campus rules
   - Photo/video consent procedures
   - Safeguarding policies
7. **Contact Information**: Leader name, phone, email

### Step 3: Submit Request in Portal

**Go to**: `/portal/permissions/new` (to be created)

**Fill in**:
- University (select from dropdown)
- Contact person (who you're submitting to)
- Contact email & phone
- Purpose (brief description)
- Proposed dates
- Upload approval letter/email (if any)
- Notes (any special requirements)

**System tracks**:
- Request date
- Status (pending/approved/rejected/expired)
- Approval date
- Expiry date (for annual renewals)

## âœ… Phase 2: Planning the Outreach Event

### Timeline: 1 week before

### Step 1: Create Event in Portal

**Go to**: `/portal/events/new`

**Fill in**:
- Title: "Campus Outreach - [University Name]"
- Description: What you'll do
- University: Select from dropdown (links to permission)
- Date & time
- Location: Specific venue on campus
- Category: "Outreach"

### Step 2: Complete Event Checklist

**In the event form, check off:**
- [ ] **Venue Booked**: Room/space confirmed
- [ ] **Sound OK**: Microphone/speakers arranged
- [ ] **Power OK**: Electricity access confirmed
- [ ] **Security OK**: Campus security notified
- [ ] **Consent Plan OK**: Photo/video consent forms ready

### Step 3: Prepare Materials

**Physical**:
- Gospel tracts (print quantity)
- Sign-up sheets (name, phone, email, interest)
- Consent forms (photo/video/name usage)
- CYSMF brochures
- QR codes (for digital sign-up)

**Digital**:
- WhatsApp group link
- Google Form for sign-ups
- Social media graphics
- Event reminder messages

### Step 4: Team Briefing

**Brief your team on**:
- Event flow & timing
- Who's doing what (MC, speaker, worship, etc.)
- Safety protocols
- Consent procedures
- Follow-up plan

## ðŸŽ¤ Phase 3: Execution Day

### Before the Event (30 mins early)

1. **Arrive early** - Set up venue
2. **Test equipment** - Sound, power, projector
3. **Welcome team** - Brief on any last-minute changes
4. **Pray together** - Commit the event to God

### During the Event

**1. Welcome & Introduction (5 mins)**
- Introduce CYSMF
- Explain what we do
- Share vision

**2. Worship & Testimonies (15-20 mins)**
- 2-3 worship songs
- 1-2 testimonies (keep it real & brief)

**3. Gospel Message (15-20 mins)**
- Clear, relevant, engaging
- Use stories & illustrations
- Address student concerns

**4. Response & Invitation (10 mins)**
- Invitation to receive Christ
- Prayer for salvation
- Invitation to join fellowship

**5. Sign-Up & Connect (10 mins)**
- Distribute tracts
- Sign-up sheets/QR code
- WhatsApp group invitation
- Take photos (with consent!)

### Metrics to Track

**Record in your phone/notebook:**
- [ ] Attendance count
- [ ] Tracts distributed
- [ ] Salvation decisions
- [ ] Sign-ups (for follow-up)
- [ ] Photos/videos taken
- [ ] Time invested (setup + event + cleanup)

## ðŸ” Safety & Compliance

### Photo/Video Consent

**Before taking photos/videos:**
1. Announce you'll be taking photos
2. Explain usage (social media, reports, website)
3. Get verbal consent or use consent forms
4. For minors, extra caution

**In the app:**
- Media is **private by default**
- Only shows on `/media` if `consent_ok = true`
- Admins can review and approve

### Safeguarding

**Always:**
- No unsolicited DMs to minors
- Use official WhatsApp groups (not private chats)
- Clear group rules
- Report any concerns to leadership

### Data Privacy

**Student data:**
- Store securely in Supabase
- Only accessible to leaders
- Never sell or share
- Delete on request

## ðŸŽ“ Campus-Specific Notes

### University of Rwanda Campuses

**3 Campuses in Kigali:**
- Nyarugenge (main campus)
- Gikondo (technical)
- Remera (business)

**Strategy:**
- Treat as separate campuses
- Different student populations
- Coordinate events (don't overlap)
- Share resources

### Private vs. Public Universities

**Private (AUCA, ALU, UNILAK, etc.):**
- Often have chaplaincy
- May be more receptive
- Smaller student body
- Easier to get permission

**Public (UR, UoK, etc.):**
- Larger student body
- More bureaucracy
- Wider reach potential
- Need strong relationships
